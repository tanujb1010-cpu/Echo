# Echo — Marketing, Monetization, Trailer & Store

## 1. Positioning
**Echo** is a *thinky, emotional* puzzle game with a hook that demos itself in 5 seconds of GIF: *you, cooperating with translucent copies of yourself, and one of them refuses to help.* The "betrayal" beat is the marketing spike — it's surprising, screenshot-able, and emotionally legible.

- **Audience:** puzzle-platformer fans (Braid, The Witness, Patrick's Parabox, Tick Tock Isle, Cosmic Express crowd), narrative-puzzle fans, accessibility-conscious players, streamers (the AI betrayals create reaction moments).
- **Comparables for store algorithms (not clones):** time/clone puzzlers + cozy-melancholy narrative puzzlers.
- **Unique selling point:** "the only co-op partner is everyone you used to be — and they're starting to have opinions."

## 2. Monetization Strategy
**Premium, no ads, no gacha, no pay-to-win.** This genre/audience rejects predatory models and they erode the contemplative tone.
- **PC (Steam):** one-time purchase, ~$14.99–$19.99. Launch discount 10–15%.
- **Mobile (Play/App Store):** **premium** with a generous **free demo (World 1)** → single IAP unlock of the full game (~$6.99–$9.99), OR a paid app. Demo-to-unlock converts better on mobile and matches the Steam demo.
- **No energy timers, no hints-for-cash** (hints are free; selling solutions would poison the design).
- **Ethical optional extras (post-launch, cosmetic/expansion only):** a soundtrack DLC, an art-book, and a substantial **content expansion** ("The Choir Below") as paid DLC. Never sell power or skips.
- **Platform cut & pricing parity** noted; regional pricing tuned via Steam/Play suggestions.
- Rationale: protect trust and word-of-mouth (the real growth engine for puzzle indies) over short-term ARPU.

## 3. Marketing Strategy & Timeline
- **T-6 months:** Steam page live (wishlists are the #1 launch lever). Devlog + dev Twitter/Bluesky/Mastodon; short GIF cadence (1 mechanic per week). Join Steam Next Fest with the World-1 demo.
- **T-3 months:** press/creator outreach with the demo + a punchy 60-sec trailer; pitch the "your clones betray you" angle. Festival submissions (Day of the Devs, IGF, Wholesome Direct given the warm tone, AbleGamers/accessibility showcases).
- **T-1 month:** influencer keys (puzzle + narrative streamers), wishlist reminder push, finalize localization, announce date.
- **Launch:** Next Fest momentum → launch discount; respond to reviews; ship a fast day-1 patch; cross-promote mobile after PC stabilizes.
- **Post-launch:** content updates, accessibility spotlight (great PR + genuinely right), the "Recursion" NG+ as a re-engagement beat, then the expansion.
- **Owned channels:** mailing list from demo, Discord community (share emergent solutions — UGC marketing), TikTok/Shorts of clone-betrayal moments.
- **KPIs:** wishlists (target curve), demo→wishlist conversion, review %, median runs-to-solve (fun proxy), refund rate, D1/D7 retention (mobile).

## 4. Trailer Concept (60–75s)
1. **0–8s — Loneliness.** A single figure in a vast empty foundry presses a plate; a door across the room she can't reach closes. *Text: "You're alone."*
2. **8–20s — The reveal.** She restarts; a translucent copy of her replays, holds the plate — she walks through. *Text: "But you've been here before."* Music blooms a second stem.
3. **20–38s — Escalation.** Rapid montage: 2, then 4, then 6 glowing Echoes building towers, passing crates, riding pendulums — a *machine of selves*. Music adds a stem per Echo (the Choir grows).
4. **38–50s — The turn.** One Echo *glances at the lever… and refuses.* Hard music cut. *Text: "They're starting to remember."* The puzzle the player "solved" falls apart.
5. **50–65s — Stakes.** Flashes of hazards, the Caretaker, a sacrifice, a faction of defiant Echoes, a hint of the Origin. *Text: "What do you owe the selves you leave behind?"*
6. **65–75s — Title + CTA.** **ECHO.** logo over a full braid solving in harmony, then one Echo turns to look at *you.* "Wishlist now on Steam." Platform logos, demo callout.
- Cut a 6s vertical loop (the refuse beat) for TikTok/Shorts and a 15s for ads.

## 5. Steam Page — Short Description
> *Every move you make is recorded forever. Restart, and your past runs return as ghostly Echoes you must cooperate with to solve impossible puzzles. But the longer your Echoes exist, the more they think for themselves — growing curious, stubborn, even treacherous. Echo is a melancholy puzzle-platformer about collaborating with everyone you used to be.*

## 6. Steam Page — Long Description (excerpt)
**BE YOUR OWN TEAM.** Solve puzzles by layering run after run — hold a door open for the *you* who comes next, catch a block your past self throws, build a ladder out of your former selves.

**YOUR PAST DEVELOPS A MIND.** Echoes begin as perfect recordings. But they evolve: some grow curious and improvise, some hesitate at danger, some refuse your orders — and a few will *sabotage* you out of spite. Earn their trust, or out-plan them.

**A FOUNDRY OF MEMORY.** Descend six strata of a dying machine that records minds, from bright glass atriums to a pre-human core. Uncover why you can't remember making some of your own Echoes.

**FEATURES**
- 50+ puzzle mechanics across 6 hand-crafted worlds.
- An evolving clone-AI: deterministic, fair, and unnervingly alive.
- 20 puzzle-boss orchestrations · 20 hazards · time-bending tools.
- 10 alternate endings shaped by how you treat your Echoes.
- Deep accessibility: full remap, assist mode, colorblind & motion options, one-handed mobile play.
- Premium. No ads. No timers. Just you, and you, and you.
