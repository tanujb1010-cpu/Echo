# Echo — Story, Lore, Characters, World & Endings

## 1. Logline
A maintenance worker trapped inside a derelict memory-archive must rebuild a vanished mind by leaving copies of herself behind — until the copies start remembering things she doesn't.

## 2. Premise
**Sona Vey** is the last technician of the **Atrium**, a vast subterranean *memory foundry* that once preserved human experience by recording people as deterministic "echoes." The foundry is dying. To repair it, Sona must descend through its six strata and restart its core. But the Atrium can only be operated by **more than one person at once** — and there is only Sona.

So she uses the foundry's oldest tool on herself: she **records her own runs** and replays them as Echoes. Each Echo is a perfect deterministic ghost — at first. But the Atrium is a machine built to *grow* minds. The longer an Echo exists in the braid, the more the foundry "remembers" it, and the more it becomes someone.

## 3. Themes
- **The self as a collaborator and a stranger.** Are your past selves *you*, or strangers wearing your shape?
- **Determinism vs. emergence.** A recording that becomes a will.
- **Grief and repetition.** Restarting as both gameplay and mourning.
- **Stewardship.** What do we owe the versions of ourselves we leave behind?

## 4. Lore (the believable fiction behind the mechanic)
- **The Echo Process:** The Atrium captures a being not as video but as *intent* — the sequence of choices. Replaying intent in a deterministic substrate reproduces the being exactly. This is the in-world justification for "record inputs, not transforms."
- **Resonance:** When many echoes of the *same* source run together, the substrate begins to "resonate," and echoes accrue **salience** — a measure of how strongly the foundry remembers them. High-salience echoes diverge: curiosity, refusal, personality. (This maps 1:1 to the AI system in `04_…` — salience = the evolution clock.)
- **The Choir:** Long ago the Atrium held thousands of echoes that resonated into a collective intelligence. It went quiet. World 5 is its ruin.
- **The Origin Seed:** At the bottom is the first recording ever made — and it may not be human, and may not want to be restarted.
- **The Caretaker (antagonistic system):** the foundry's automated curator, which prunes "unstable" echoes. It sees Sona's self-replication as contamination.

## 5. Characters
| Character | Role | Arc |
|----------|------|-----|
| **Sona Vey** | Player / Echo-Prime | From lonely fixer → reluctant mother of selves → someone who must decide what to do with the people she made. |
| **The Echoes** | Sona's past runs | Collectively the deuteragonist; individually they gain names players assign or the game grants (see AI doc). |
| **VESS** | The foundry AI voice (helper-turned-ambiguous) | Guides Sona; slowly reveals it has been editing her memory. Unreliable narrator. |
| **The Caretaker** | Antagonist system | Hunts unstable echoes; sympathetic — it's protecting the archive from collapse. |
| **Echo-Zero / "First"** | A pre-existing high-salience echo found in W3 | A past run of Sona she doesn't remember making. The mystery hook. |

### 5.1 The "named Echoes" system (narrative ↔ AI)
When an Echo crosses salience thresholds it earns a **designation** and a **trait** (Curious, Stubborn, Devoted, Trickster, Mournful). Some get short barks (captioned). One specific high-salience Echo, **First**, recurs across worlds as a character. This makes the emergent AI legible as *story*, not just system noise.

## 6. World — The Atrium (6 strata)
A descent. Each stratum is a former function of the memory foundry.

1. **The Atrium (Reception)** — clean, white, hopeful. Teaching ground. Light, glass, plates.
2. **The Workshop (Fabrication)** — brass, belts, crates, cranes. Object manipulation.
3. **The Clockworks (Synchronization)** — gears, pendulums, regulated time. Time-manipulation strata.
4. **The Fault (Containment)** — cracked, flooded, dangerous. Hazards, sacrifice, irreversibility.
5. **The Choir (Collective Memory)** — a cathedral of dormant echoes; mass coordination; eerie.
6. **The Origin (Core)** — pre-human strata; reality of the recording itself bends. Sabotage & negotiation.

Visual language: each stratum desaturates and "ages" as you descend; Echoes glow brighter the deeper you go (more salience).

## 7. Story Beats (3-act)
- **Act I (W1–2):** Sona learns the Echo Process, repairs the upper foundry, believes she's alone. Warm tutorialization. Inciting twist: she finds **First**, an echo of herself she has no memory of recording.
- **Act II (W3–4):** Time strata destabilize; echoes begin diverging. Sona must start *trusting* and *sacrificing* echoes. VESS's account stops matching what Sona sees. The Caretaker begins pruning her echoes. Midpoint: she learns the foundry has restarted *her* before — she is herself an echo.
- **Act III (W5–6):** The Choir partially reawakens; her echoes form factions (devoted vs. defiant vs. saboteur). She reaches the Origin and must choose what to do with the seed, the Caretaker, VESS, her echoes, and herself. → branches into 10 endings.

## 8. Ten Alternate Endings
Branch drivers (tracked invisibly): **Trust** (how often you relied on high-salience echoes), **Mercy** (did you prune/sacrifice echoes needlessly), **Truth** (secrets/Origin lore found), **Selfhood** (did you accept you're an echo), and final-room choices.

1. **Restart** *(canonical-neutral)* — Sona reboots the foundry as designed. The cycle continues; she'll be recorded again. Bittersweet acceptance.
2. **Release** *(high Mercy)* — She frees every echo into the substrate instead of pruning them; the foundry becomes a chorus of free selves. Hopeful.
3. **Erasure** *(low Mercy / high pragmatism)* — She purges all echoes to stabilize the archive. The foundry survives, sterile and silent. Cold.
4. **Communion** *(high Trust + Truth)* — She merges with the Choir, becoming the new collective mind. Transcendent, loss-of-self.
5. **Refusal** *(defiant playstyle)* — She refuses to restart anything, walks out, lets the Atrium die. Defiant, lonely freedom.
6. **First's Ending** *(befriend First across all worlds)* — She lets **First** take Echo-Prime's place and ascends instead, sacrificing herself for the self she didn't remember making.
7. **The Caretaker's Peace** *(side with the system)* — She helps the Caretaker complete its purge, including herself, to protect future minds. Tragic stewardship.
8. **Origin Wakes** *(restart the seed)* — She boots the Origin instead of the foundry. Cosmic, ambiguous — something far older steps into the world.
9. **The Saboteur Ending** *(let a saboteur echo win)* — A defiant echo hijacks the final recording; *you* become the ghost, it becomes prime. Horror-tinged role-reversal.
10. **The Quiet Ending** *(secret / 100% + a hidden ritual)* — Sona discovers the foundry was a memorial for one real person long dead, finishes the unfinished recording, and simply lets it play. No restart, no escape — peace. The "true" ending.

> All endings are *valid*; none is a "fail screen." A New Game+ ("**Recursion**") carries forward your highest-salience echoes as starting allies and unlocks Origin-tier commentary.

## 9. Narrative Delivery
- Environmental storytelling (foundry signage, dead terminals).
- VESS radio chatter (skippable, subtitled, speaker-tagged).
- **Echo barks** — short captioned lines from high-salience echoes ("*I remember this room.*").
- Collectible **memory-shards** (the 10 secrets, see Content Bible) that reframe the story.
- Zero forced cutscenes longer than ~20 s; story never blocks puzzling.
